Virtual Event Management

Virtual event management is the operating layer behind a calm online event. It connects strategy, speaker readiness, platform setup, audience flow, moderation, and live production control.

Get production helpEmail scooter@westpeek.ventures

Definition

Virtual event management covers the planning and execution work required to make an online or hybrid event run smoothly. It is broader than platform setup and narrower than general marketing. It is the bridge between event objective, audience experience, production reality, and day-of-show control.

Core workstreams

  • Audience and event objective
  • Format and agenda design
  • Speaker onboarding and tech checks
  • Run-of-show and cue sheet
  • Platform stack and backup plan
  • Registration, reminders, and access
  • Moderation, chat, Q&A, and sponsor moments
  • Recording, recap, and post-event content

What separates management from software

Software gives you rooms, links, streams, and buttons. Management gives you decisions, owners, timing, roles, escalation paths, rehearsals, and a clear answer to “what happens if this breaks?”
