How much does virtual event production cost?

There is no single standard virtual event production price because the cost depends on the risk, complexity, and amount of real production ownership involved.

Short answer

Virtual event production can range from light-touch webinar support to full-service conference execution. Cost usually depends on the number of speakers, amount of rehearsal required, custom graphics, complexity of audience interaction, platform choice, and how much live decision-making the production team owns. A low-stakes internal call is not priced like a high-visibility external event.

What drives the number

The main drivers are format complexity, live audience risk, technical layers, show length, number of stakeholders, and how much has to be built from scratch. Teams also pay for confidence: if a provider is removing real risk, rehearsing properly, and owning the live operating layer, the price reflects that responsibility.

What buyers get wrong

Buyers often compare software cost to production cost and think they are substitutes. They are not. Buying a webinar platform does not buy rehearsal discipline, live cueing, speaker management, or calm execution. Another mistake is under-scoping pre-production and then acting surprised when the event feels improvised.

A better way to compare providers

Instead of only asking for a number, compare who owns the run-of-show, how rehearsals are handled, what happens if a speaker slips, how sponsor or stakeholder requirements are managed, and what deliverables exist after the event. Those factors are much closer to the real value than the raw fee alone.
